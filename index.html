About

What is The Equal Lens?

The Equal Lens is a youth initiative that uses games, stories and guided conversations to make gender equality real and practical for children – not just a chapter in a book.

Instead of telling students what to think, we invite them to notice:

  • Who speaks more in class? Who is interrupted?
  • Who is expected to do what at home?
  • What jokes are “normal” – and who gets left out?

Through simple activities, we help them try new, fairer ways of behaving – without shame, blame or big speeches.

Sample activities

What does an Equal Lens session feel like?

Some examples of low-tech games and prompts we might use in a workshop. Click on any card to reveal how the activity connects back to gender equality.

Game
“Who gets the chair?”

Students stand in a circle with one less chair. The prompt changes each round: “If you love blue, grab a chair”, “If you’ve ever been told ‘girls don’t…’ sit down”.

We discuss who moves first, who hesitates, and how rules quietly push some people out.

Story circle
“That’s not for you”

Short story of two friends told twice – once with swapped genders. Students note what feels “believable” and why.

We unpack how our ideas of “normal” are gendered, and where we learned those ideas.

Reflection
Invisible work map

Kids draw a simple map of their home and list all the daily tasks: cooking, cleaning, earning, caring, fixing.

They mark who usually does what, then re-design a fairer version together.

Quick check-in

A tiny gender equality quiz

This is not an exam – just a nudge to notice how we think. You can use this in class as a warm-up or closing activity, and invite students to explain their choices.

Question 1 of 3

How to use this in a workshop

  • Ask students to answer silently first, then discuss.
  • Invite them to defend answers with “because…” rather than just “right/wrong”.
  • Connect every answer back to real-life situations they recognise.
  • End by asking: “What can we do differently from today?”
