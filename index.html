Overview

Seeing gender
differently

The Equal Lens is a playful initiative that helps young people notice gender, power and fairness in everyday life — without turning it into a heavy lecture.

Visual-first We start with pictures, prompts and stories instead of long explanations.
Low-tech All activities work with just chart paper, pens and a bit of space.
Age 10+ Designed for middle-schoolers and mixed youth groups.
No shaming We focus on curiosity and reflection, not blaming individuals.
In a session

What young people
actually do

A session is not a speech. It feels like a mix of games, group work and honest conversations.

Play Line games, sorting exercises and role-plays that surface silent rules.
Notice Map who does which chores, who gets curfews and who is “allowed” to feel.
Listen Hear classmates share experiences they usually keep inside.
Name it Find simple, honest words for fairness, respect and boundaries.
Everyday spaces

Where patterns
show up

The Equal Lens helps students connect big words like “gender equality” to ordinary places in their own lives.

At home Who cooks, who “helps”, who decides what is safe or unsafe?
In school Who is interrupted, who is picked as leader, who is teased for being “different”?
With friends Who gets called “bossy”, “weak”, “too emotional” or “too much”?
Online How jokes, memes and comments quietly teach us what is “normal”.
What can shift

What we hope
opens up

The goal is not perfection. It’s one small mindset shift, one tiny action that feels possible this week.

Language Students learn kinder, clearer ways to say “this feels unfair”.
Confidence Quieter voices find more room; louder voices learn to make space.
Shared work Chores, care work and responsibilities become a bit more balanced.
Empathy Students see gender as everyone’s issue, not just a “girls’ topic”.
Start here

Bring The Equal Lens
into your space

Whether you’re a school, NGO, youth group or just one motivated adult, you can start small and try one thing.

For schools Use a single activity in a life-skills period or as part of a theme day.
For NGOs Blend Equal Lens tools into your existing gender or adolescent programmes.
For youth clubs Run one game + discussion in any safe space where young people hang out.
For individuals Try the quiz, make a pledge, or adapt a question for your own context.
